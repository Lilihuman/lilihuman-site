This is the class structure I come back to again and again. It works for beginners, it works for regulars, and it works for the days when you only have yourself and a living room floor.

45 minutes. Six songs. One very sweaty, very happy group of humans.

Here's exactly how it goes.

---

## 1. Warm-Up (5–7 minutes)

Goal: gradually raise your heart rate and loosen your joints. Keep the choreography simple here — you want people to feel confident early.

**Song 1 – Easy Latin Groove**

- March in place
- Step touch side to side
- Shoulder rolls
- Hip circles
- Arm swings

**Song 2 – Light Dance Moves**

- Grapevine right and left
- Salsa basic step
- Body rolls
- Light squats with arm reaches

---

## 2. Dance Cardio (15 minutes)

This is where the magic happens. Three songs, three different vibes.

**Song 3 – Salsa / Latin Beat**

- Salsa basic
- Salsa forward and back
- Hip shake
- Turn step
- Add: fast arms and double time feet

**Song 4 – Reggaeton**

- Step + squat
- Punch arms forward
- Side shuffle
- Booty shake
- Fitness add-on: 8 squat pulses, 8 jump squats (optional)

**Song 5 – Merengue**

- March hips
- Side step with arms
- Travel forward and back
- Knee lifts
- Fitness add-on: fast high knees for 20 seconds

---

## 3. Fitness Segment (10 minutes)

We switch from dance to strength and toning. Repeat this circuit twice with 30 seconds rest between rounds.

**1. Squats — 1 minute**
Squat + arm reach, squat + hip circle

**2. Core — 1 minute**
Standing oblique crunch, knee to elbow

**3. Cardio Burst — 1 minute**
Jumping jacks OR fast salsa feet

**4. Glutes — 1 minute**
Back lunges, kickbacks

---

## 4. Final Dance Party (5 minutes)

This is everyone's favourite part. Song 6 — Latin pop or a Zumba favourite.

- Big grapevines
- Body rolls
- Jump turns
- Freestyle

Tell your people: just dance. Energy matters more than perfect choreography. Always.

---

## 5. Cool Down + Stretch (5 minutes)

Slow song. Breathe. You earned this.

Stretch through: hamstrings, quads, glutes, shoulders, neck. Finish with deep breathing and let the endorphins do their thing.

---

## My go-to playlist

These songs work beautifully for a Zumba class:

- Danza Kuduro
- Bailando
- Mi Gente
- Taki Taki
- Waka Waka

---

## Teaching tips (especially for your first class)

Teach in 8-count patterns — that's the standard Zumba format. Repeat moves 3–4 times before you change them. Use big arm movements to guide people. And smile — your energy is contagious, and it matters more than perfect choreography.

My favourite cues: *"Let's go!" "Four more!" "You got it!" "Dance break!"*

That last one gets people every time.
