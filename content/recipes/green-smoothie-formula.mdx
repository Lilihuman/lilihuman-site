I've tried a lot of green smoothies. Most of them taste like a field. This one actually tastes good, and I've been making some version of it basically every morning for two years. It's a formula more than a fixed recipe — swap things based on what you have.

## The Formula

**1 cup liquid** + **1 big handful greens** + **1–2 cups frozen fruit** + **1 booster** + **sweetener if needed**

## My Go-To Version

- 1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or coconut water)
- 2 large handfuls baby spinach
- 1 frozen banana
- ½ cup frozen mango
- 1 tbsp almond butter
- ½ tsp vanilla extract

## Method

1. Add the liquid first, then the greens, then everything else.
2. Blend on high for 45–60 seconds until completely smooth.
3. Taste and add a little honey or another piece of fruit if you want it sweeter.
4. Drink immediately or pour into a jar and refrigerate for up to 8 hours.

## Booster Ideas

- **Protein:** a scoop of protein powder, Greek yogurt, or hemp seeds
- **Healthy fat:** almond butter, avocado, or a tablespoon of chia seeds
- **Anti-inflammatory:** a thumb of fresh ginger or ½ tsp turmeric
- **Fibre:** a tablespoon of ground flaxseed

## Notes

Frozen banana is the key to that creamy, thick texture without needing ice. Peel and freeze overripe bananas in a zip-lock bag and you'll always have them on hand.
